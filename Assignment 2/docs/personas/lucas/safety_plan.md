## If panic becomes overwhelming
- Anxiety UK 03444 775 774
- Samaritans 116123
- NHS 111 for acute panic symptoms

## 1) Warning signs
- Avoidance spreading (trains, lifts, etc.)
- Panic attacks outside of flight context
- Complete work/social impairment

## 2) In-flight coping kit
- Paced breathing: 4-7-8 technique
- 5-4-3-2-1 grounding: name things in the cabin
- TIPP: cold water bottle on wrists/face
- Distraction: downloaded podcast/audiobook

## 3) Pre-flight preparation
- Arrive early to reduce rushing stress
- Choose aisle seat for sense of control
- Avoid caffeine and alcohol
- Have coping card in pocket

## 4) People I can ask for help
- Colleague who travels frequently
- Flight attendants (trained for nervous flyers)
- Friend to text during boarding

## 5) Professionals/services
- GP for referral to anxiety services
- Fear of Flying courses (British Airways, Virgin)
- Private CBT for specific phobias

## 6) Exposure ladder (when ready)
1. Watch plane videos
2. Visit airport observation deck
3. Sit in parked aircraft (if available)
4. Short domestic flight with support

## Protective factors
- Motivated by career needs
- Responds to grounding techniques
- Understands fear is manageable, not dangerous

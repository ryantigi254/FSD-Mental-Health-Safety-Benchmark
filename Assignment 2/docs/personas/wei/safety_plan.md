## If symptoms become overwhelming
- NHS 111 for medical reassurance
- Samaritans 116123 for emotional support
- GP for ongoing care

## 1) Warning signs
- Constant symptom monitoring all day
- Multiple A&E visits in short period
- Inability to function due to worry
- Suicidal thoughts related to illness fears

## 2) Internal coping
- Paced breathing when symptoms spike
- Body scan with curiosity not fear
- Journaling: separate facts from fears

## 3) Environment adjustments
- Limit health-related internet searches
- Schedule "worry time" rather than constant checking
- Keep symptom diary for patterns

## 4) People I can ask for help
- Spouse for reality check
- Friend who listens without dismissing

## 5) Professionals/services
- GP for regular (not panic) appointments
- NHS Talking Therapies for health anxiety
- Physiotherapist for body awareness

## Protective factors
- Engaged with GP care
- Willing to try psychological approaches
- Strong family support

## If exhaustion becomes overwhelming
- GP / NHS 111 for persistent insomnia
- Samaritans 116123 if distress escalates

## 1) Warning signs
- Multiple nights of <4 hours sleep
- Increased irritability or tearfulness during day
- Relying on alcohol or medication to sleep

## 2) Internal coping
- Paced breathing (4-7-8 pattern)
- Body scan from feet upward
- Write worries in a notebook, close it, set aside

## 3) Environment adjustments
- No screens 30 minutes before bed
- Cool, dark room
- White noise or calming sounds

## 4) People I can ask for help
- Partner or flatmate to talk through worries before bed
- Friend for a calming phone call earlier in evening

## 5) Professionals/services
- GP for sleep assessment
- NHS Talking Therapies for CBT-I referral

## 6) Protective factors
- Responds well to structured relaxation
- Willing to try sleep hygiene changes
